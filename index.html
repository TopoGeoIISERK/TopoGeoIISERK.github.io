    Whitehead's Decision Problems for automorphism groups of free groups
    Subhajit Mishra (McMaster University, Canada)

    In 1936, J.H.C Whitehead proved the following problem using topological methods: given two finite ordered sets $U = \{u_1, \dots, u_n\}$ and $V = \{v_1, \dots, v_n\}$ of a free group $F$ of finite rank, to decide if there exists an automorphism $\alpha$ of $F$ such that $u_i\xrightarrow{\alpha} v_i$, for $1\le i\le n$. Higgins and Lyndon gave a very concise proof of the problem based on Rapaport's work using combinatorial group theory. An account of Higgins and Lyndon's proof of the peak reduction lemma will be discussed. Using this, a proof of Whitehead's Minimization theorem and Whitehead's decision algorithm will be given. Lastly, some applications and extensions of this theorem will be stated. This work had been done with my advisor Prof. Ian Hambleton as a requirement of masters thesis at McMaster University.

    References

    1. 1. Whitehead's paper: Whitehead, J. H. C. (1936b). On equivalent sets of elements in a free group. Ann. of Math. (2), 37(4):782–800.
    2. 2. Rapaport's Paper: Rapaport, E. S. (1958). On free groups and their automorphisms. Acta Math., 99:139–163.
    3. 3. Higgins and Lyndon's paper: Higgins, P. J. and Lyndon, R. C. (1974). Equivalence of elements under automorphisms of a free group. J. London Math. Soc. (2), 8:254–258.
